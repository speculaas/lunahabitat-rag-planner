# Crew Time Budget Policy

## Purpose

This document defines how daily crew time should be allocated across lunar habitat operations.

## Baseline Allocation Rules

- Reserve the first portion of the day for life-support and habitat-safety checks.
- Fit non-critical expansion tasks only after high-priority diagnostics are scheduled.
- Leave contingency room in the daily plan for interruptions or follow-up inspections.

## Priority Ordering

1. life-support and pressure integrity
2. emergency readiness and response
3. construction safety and shielding continuity
4. greenhouse and monitoring work
5. routine communications and low-priority housekeeping

## Time Budget Guidance

- If the daily crew budget is under 120 minutes, include only critical and high-priority tasks.
- If the budget is between 120 and 180 minutes, include one medium-priority monitoring or communications task after critical work.
- If the budget exceeds 180 minutes, expansion tasks may be added if prerequisites are satisfied.

## Warning Conditions

- Warn when more than one low-priority task displaces a critical inspection.
- Warn when no contingency time remains in the schedule.
