# Lunar Communications Windows

## Purpose

This document defines preferred scheduling rules for lunar habitat communications tasks.

## Standard Guidance

- Schedule primary uplinks during the afternoon operations window when possible.
- Reserve at least one uninterrupted communications block for mission updates and diagnostics.
- Treat high-priority distress or emergency traffic as preemptive over routine maintenance summaries.

## Scheduling Constraints

- Avoid overlapping critical uplinks with high-noise construction work.
- Avoid scheduling routine communications during emergency response drills.
- If crew time is limited, prioritize operationally critical uplinks over status-report clean-up tasks.

## Priority Rules

- Distress, safety, or life-support communications are high priority.
- Routine telemetry uploads are medium priority unless paired with a safety review.
- Non-essential outreach or archive sync tasks are low priority.

## Warning Conditions

- If the communications block is interrupted by construction noise, flag the plan for revision.
- If no dedicated communications window is present, warn that uplink reliability may decrease.
