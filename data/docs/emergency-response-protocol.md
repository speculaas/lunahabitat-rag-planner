# Emergency Response Protocol

## Purpose

This document defines immediate scheduling and prioritization rules during habitat emergencies.

## Trigger Conditions

- pressure leak alert
- oxygen recycler failure
- communications outage during a critical mission window
- fire, contamination, or structural instability warning

## Emergency Scheduling Rules

- Suspend non-critical construction and routine monitoring tasks during an active emergency.
- Elevate emergency diagnostics and communications to the highest priority.
- Reserve crew time for evacuation checks, seal confirmation, and status relay tasks.

## Communications Guidance

- Send emergency status updates before routine telemetry or archive transfers.
- Keep one communications channel dedicated to mission control coordination if available.

## Recovery Rules

- Do not resume expansion work until the emergency source is identified and cleared.
- Require a follow-up systems inspection after any life-support or pressure incident.

## Warning Conditions

- If an emergency trigger is present and the plan still contains low-priority work, flag the schedule as unsafe.
- If no communication task is scheduled during a critical incident, require manual intervention.
